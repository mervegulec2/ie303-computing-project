============================================================
CONNECTION: Water Jug Problem ↔ Linear Diophantine Equations
============================================================
The water jug problem is mathematically equivalent to solving:
a₁×x₁ + a₂×x₂ = b

Where:
- a₁, a₂ are jug capacities (3 and 5 gallons)
- b is the target amount (4 gallons)
- x₁, x₂ are integer variables representing:
  * x₁: net effect of operations on the 3-gallon jug
  * x₂: net effect of operations on the 5-gallon jug

Each pouring operation corresponds to adding/subtracting jug capacities:
- Filling jug: +capacity
- Emptying jug: -current_amount
- Pouring A→B: -amount_from_A, +amount_to_B

The equation has integer solutions iff b is a multiple of GCD(a₁,a₂).
For a₁=3, a₂=5: GCD(3,5)=1, so any integer b is achievable.
For a₁=976, a₂=1224: we need to find GCD(976,1224).

SUMMARY

GCD(976, 1224) = 8
